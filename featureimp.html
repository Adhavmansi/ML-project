Feature Importance in Water Quality Prediction

Understanding the key features that influence water quality is essential for effective water management and ensuring the safety of drinking water. Here we break down the nine crucial parameters used in our water quality prediction model.

1. pH

The pH level of water indicates its acidity or alkalinity. It is measured on a scale from 0 to 14, where 7 is neutral, values below 7 are acidic, and values above 7 are alkaline. Maintaining the right pH is vital for the health of aquatic life and the safety of drinking water.

2. Hardness

Hardness measures the concentration of calcium and magnesium in water. It affects the water's ability to lather with soap and can cause scaling in pipes and appliances. Hardness is measured in mg/L, with higher values indicating harder water.

3. Solids

Total dissolved solids (TDS) refer to the combined content of all inorganic and organic substances contained in a liquid. High levels of solids can affect the taste of water and may indicate the presence of harmful contaminants.

4. Chloramines

Chloramines are disinfectants used to treat drinking water. While they help control harmful bacteria and viruses, high levels of chloramines can lead to an unpleasant taste and potential health risks.

5. Sulfate

Sulfate is a naturally occurring substance found in minerals, soil, and rocks. High levels of sulfate in drinking water can lead to a laxative effect and a bitter taste, making it important to monitor.

6. Conductivity

Conductivity measures water's ability to conduct an electrical current, which is directly related to the concentration of ions in the water. High conductivity can indicate a high concentration of dissolved salts, which may affect water quality.

7. Organic Carbon

Organic carbon is a measure of the organic compounds present in water. High levels of organic carbon can lead to the formation of disinfection byproducts during water treatment, which can be harmful to health.

8. Trihalomethanes

Trihalomethanes (THMs) are a group of chemicals formed as a byproduct when chlorine is used to disinfect water. High levels of THMs can increase the risk of cancer and other health issues, making it important to control their levels.

9. Turbidity

Turbidity measures the clarity of water, with high turbidity indicating the presence of suspended particles. Turbidity can interfere with disinfection and provide a medium for microbial growth, thus impacting water quality.

By monitoring these key features, we can predict and ensure the quality of water, helping to safeguard public health and the environment.
